Soldier Brief

(Briefing NCO stands before the formation)

Alright, listen up. My name is [Rank/Name], and I am with the DPC Mayor Cell. On behalf of the 21st STB, welcome to the Deployment Processing Center at Rhine Ordnance Barracks. My job is to give you the information you need to operate here safely and efficiently. Pay attention.

1. In-Processing Flow

In a few moments, you will be directed to our in-processing line inside Building 235. You will move through the S1 stations to establish accountability and receive your billeting assignments. Follow the instructions from the staff, have your CAC card ready, and move with a sense of purpose.

2. Key Facility Locations

  • Mayor Cell: Your primary point of contact for any and all issues is located in Building 230.
  • Building 231: This is the Family Transition Building. It is off-limits to all transient personnel unless specifically authorized.
  • H2F Facility and DFAC: These facilities are available for your use. Your leadership will provide times and locations.
  • AAFES Shoppette: The local Shoppette is available and located within walking distance from the DPC.

3. Security & Operational Security (OPSEC)

This is the most important part of this brief.

  • We are currently at FPCON [Current Level]. Maintain situational awareness at all times.
  • This is a transition site. What you see here, what you hear here, and who you see here, stays here.
  • Operational Security is not a suggestion; it is a direct order.
  • You are prohibited from taking pictures or videos of personnel, equipment, or facilities within the DPC.
  • Do not post, share, or advertise unit movements on social media or any other forum — this includes your own movement.

4. Rules & Restrictions

  • General Order Number 1 is in effect. There is no consumption of alcohol.
  • You are NOT authorized to leave the installation to go into the local community.
  • Authorized Travel: With approval from your unit leadership, you may use on-post bus or authorized taxi services to travel to the Ramstein Air Base Main PX.
  • If you travel to Ramstein, you must be back inside the DPC and signed in no later than 1830. No exceptions.

5. Senior Leader Link-Up

Finally, I need the senior leader — the ranking officer or NCO — of the transitioning units to identify themselves to me at the conclusion of this brief. We need your name and contact information to establish a clear line of communication between your unit and the Mayor Cell.

That is all I have. Your safety and the security of this mission are our top priorities. Follow the rules, listen to your leadership, and use your chain of command.

Are there any immediate questions?

Welcome to the DPC.

Civilian Brief

1. Purpose of the Mayor Cell

The Mayor Cell is your primary point of contact during your stay. Our mission is to:

  • Organize and manage your movement through the DPC.
  • Maintain accountability of all personnel.
  • Connect you with any necessary personnel or support services.
  • Coordinate and organize your transportation to the airport for onward travel.

2. Accountability & Check-In

To ensure we can support you effectively, we require all personnel to sign in with the Mayor Cell. Immediately following this brief, please see a member of our team to provide your contact information and a primary point of contact (POC) for your group or organization. The Mayor Cell is located in Building 230 for any administrative needs.

3. Billeting & Facilities

  • Billeting: Your assigned lodging is in Building 231.
  • Restrooms: Bathroom facilities are located directly across from Building 231.
  • Dining: The Dining Facility (DFAC) and the post Shoppette are available for your use.
  • Area of Operations: While you are here, all personnel are restricted to the footprint of Rhine Ordnance Barracks. You are not authorized to leave the installation for the duration of your stay.

4. Medical & Emergency Services

  • Medical Aid Station: For non-emergency medical needs, a Medical Aid Station is available 24/7 in Building 235.
  • Behavioral Health: Behavioral health services are also available should you need them.
  • Medical Emergencies: In the event of a serious medical emergency, dial 112. A local ambulance will respond to provide immediate services.

5. Standards & Conduct

  • Alcohol: Please be advised that General Order Number 1 is in effect for all personnel at the DPC, which prohibits the consumption of alcoholic beverages.
  • Professionalism: We ask that you maintain a professional bearing at all times. You are representing the U.S. Government to our Host Nation partners.

6. Operational Security (OPSEC)

  • Social Media: Do not post any details about your current location, travel itineraries, timelines, or mission-related activities on any social media platforms.
  • Awareness: Be mindful of your conversations in public spaces, as you never know who might be listening.

Unit Point of Contact Guidance

Unit leadership or designated representatives should coordinate directly with the Mayor Cell to ensure accountability and transportation coordination.

  • Ensure your personnel sign in upon arrival.
  • Provide updated personnel rosters if changes occur.
  • Maintain communication with the Mayor Cell regarding movement timelines.
  • Coordinate transportation and flight timelines through the Mayor Cell.
